Camping Checklist

The Ultimate Camping Checklist for Beginners (UK Edition)

Don't get caught out by the weather or forget a crucial piece of kit! This comprehensive guide covers everything you need for a safe, comfortable, and enjoyable camping trip in the UK. Whether you're planning your first camping adventure or looking to refine your packing strategy, this guide will help ensure you're well-prepared for British conditions.

Essential Shelter & Sleeping Gear

  • Tent: Choose a 3-season tent with a minimum 2000mm hydrostatic head rating. Look for features like:
    • Double-skin design to prevent condensation
    • Fully taped seams and quality groundsheet
    • Good ventilation options
    • Guy lines and pegs for windy conditions
  • Groundsheet: A footprint or groundsheet protector prevents tent floor from wear and moisture. Consider:
    • Using a footprint slightly smaller than your tent
    • Always keep it packed for purchase
  • Sleeping Bag: Choose based on the season:
    • Summer: 2-3 season (comfort rating around 5°C)
    • Spring/Autumn: 3-4 season (comfort rating around 0°C)
    • Winter: 4 season (comfort rating -5°C or lower)
  • Sleeping Mat: Essential for insulation and comfort:
    • Self-inflating or air mats for comfort
    • Closed cell foam mats for durability and warmth
    • Look for R-value of 2-3+ for 3-season camping
  • Pillow: Inflatable or compressible camping pillow for neck support
  • Mallet: For securing tent pegs in hard ground

Clothing System

Layering is key for UK weather. Pack clothes that can be combined for different conditions.

  • Base Layers:
    • Merino wool or synthetic tops and bottoms
    • Quick-drying underwear and socks
    • Avoid cotton as it retains moisture
  • Mid Layers:
    • Fleece or down jacket for insulation
    • Lightweight hiking trousers
    • Long-sleeve shirts for sun protection
  • Outer Layers:
    • Waterproof jacket and overtrousers
    • Waterproof gaiters for boots or shoes
  • Footwear:
    • Hiking boots or trail shoes
    • Camp shoes or sandals
    • Warm hat and gloves
    • Buff or neck gaiter

Cooking & Food Equipment

Stay well-fed and hydrated with the right cooking gear.

  • Stove System:
    • Gas stove: Simple and wind protection
    • Fuel canisters (check compatibility)
    • Liquid fuel or multi-fuel (for winter/expeditions)
  • Cookware:
    • Lightweight bowl and pot (or mess kit)
    • Mug and spork (or cutlery set)
  • Food: Freeze-dried or portioned rations
  • Water: Bottles or purification tablets
  • Snacks: Energy bars, nuts, dried fruit

Other Essentials

  • Headtorch/Flashlight: With spare batteries
  • First Aid Kit: Include blister plasters and personal meds
  • Map & Compass: Or GPS device (with spare batteries)
  • Multi-tool or knife
  • Toiletries: Biodegradable soap, toothbrush, toothpaste, toilet paper
  • Rubbish bags: Leave no trace!
  • Insect repellent & sunscreen
  • Power bank
  • Notebook & pen

Optional Extras

  • Camera/binoculars
  • Camp chair
  • Book or cards
  • Firelighter/matches/lighter
  • Small repair kit (duct tape, sewing kit)

Tip: Always check the weather forecast and adapt your kit accordingly. Practice pitching your tent and using your stove before your trip!
