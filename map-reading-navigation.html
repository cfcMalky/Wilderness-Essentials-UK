Navigation & Map Reading

Introduction

Navigation and map reading are essential skills for anyone venturing into the wilderness. Whether you're a seasoned hiker or a beginner camper, understanding how to read a map and use a compass can ensure your safety and enhance your outdoor experience. This guide covers the basics of map reading and compass navigation, helping you confidently explore the great outdoors.

Core Map Reading Skills

Understanding Map Symbols and Legends

  • Blue lines for rivers and streams.
  • Green areas for forests or woods.
  • Black lines for roads and paths.
  • Icons for campsites, viewpoints, and car parks.

Always check the legend on your map before setting out.

Orienting Your Map

Lay your map flat and align it with the terrain around you, using a compass if possible. This helps you visualize your route and spot landmarks.

Using Grid References

UK maps use blue grid lines to pinpoint locations. To give a grid reference:

  1. Read the number along the bottom (eastings).
  2. Read the number up the side (northings).
  3. Combine for a four-figure (general area) or six-figure (precise point) reference. Example: 3311.

Understanding Contour Lines

  • Close contour lines mean steep slopes.
  • Wide contour lines mean gentle slopes.
  • Concentric circles = hills or summits.
  • V-shaped contours = valleys (the point usually faces upstream).

Measuring Distance and Estimating Time

  • Use the map’s scale bar or a ruler for straight lines, or string for winding paths.
  • Estimate time using Naismith’s Rule: about 1 hour per 5km (3 miles), plus 1 hour for every 600m (2,000 feet) climbed.

Using a Compass

  • Baseplate: Clear mount for map visibility.
  • Compass Housing: Rotating dial with compass points.
  • Magnetic Needle: Red end points to magnetic north.
  • Orienting Lines: For aligning with map grid lines.
  • Orienting Arrow: Shows north on the housing.
  • Index Line: Marks the bearing to travel.
  • Direction of Travel Arrow: Indicates your route.

How to Take a Bearing

  1. Place the compass edge along your route on the map.
  2. Rotate the housing until orienting lines match the map’s north-south grid, with the orienting arrow pointing north.
  3. Add/subtract magnetic declination (check your map for the value).
  4. Hold the compass flat and turn your body until the needle aligns with the orienting arrow. The travel arrow now shows your direction.

Navigation Tips

  • Practice: Try following a route at home or in your local park before heading out.
  • Use Landmarks: Match features on your map to the landscape around you.
  • Stay Updated: Magnetic north shifts slowly over time. Check your map for the current declination.
  • Always Carry a Backup: Bring a paper map and compass, even if you have GPS.
